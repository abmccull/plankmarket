# Regional Closeout Flooring: Why Location Matters for Freight

A 40% discount on closeout flooring means nothing if freight costs 30%.

Flooring is heavy. A pallet of hardwood weighs 1,500-2,500 lbs. A pallet of tile can exceed 3,000 lbs. Shipping these products across the country isn't cheap, and freight costs can turn a great deal into an average one.

This guide helps you evaluate closeout deals with freight in mind.

## Freight Cost Realities

Flooring freight costs depend on three factors: weight, distance, and method.

### Weight

- **Solid hardwood** — 2.5-3.5 lbs/sq ft, 1,500-2,000 lbs per pallet
- **Engineered hardwood** — 2-3 lbs/sq ft, 1,200-1,800 lbs per pallet
- **LVP** — 1.5-2.5 lbs/sq ft, 900-1,500 lbs per pallet
- **Laminate** — 1-1.5 lbs/sq ft, 600-900 lbs per pallet
- **Ceramic tile** — 3-4 lbs/sq ft, 2,000-2,800 lbs per pallet
- **Porcelain tile** — 4-5 lbs/sq ft, 2,500-3,500 lbs per pallet

Heavier products cost more to ship. Tile and solid hardwood have the highest freight burden.

### Distance

Freight scales with distance, but not linearly:

- **Under 200 miles** — $0.20-0.50/sq ft
- **200-500 miles** — $0.40-0.80/sq ft
- **500-1,000 miles** — $0.70-1.20/sq ft
- **1,000-1,500 miles** — $1.00-1.60/sq ft
- **1,500+ miles** — $1.40-2.00+/sq ft

Cross-country shipping can add $1.50-2.50/sq ft to your cost. That's often 30-50% of the product cost for value flooring.

### Shipping Method

**LTL (Less Than Truckload)** — Most closeout purchases. Flexible quantity, reasonable rates.

**Full Truckload** — Large orders (20,000+ sq ft). Lower per-sq-ft cost, but requires volume.

**Will-call/Pickup** — Local purchases. No freight cost at all.

**Parcel** — Small samples only. Not economical for actual flooring orders.

Full truckload rates are 30-50% lower per sq ft than LTL, but require volume.

## The Regional Advantage

This is why local closeouts matter.

**Same product, same discount, different locations:**

Consider the same hardwood closeout at $2.50/sq ft from two sellers:

**Closeout 100 miles away:** $2.50 product + $0.30 freight = **$2.80/sq ft delivered.** Clear win.

**Closeout 800 miles away:** $2.50 product + $1.00 freight = **$3.50/sq ft delivered.** Barely beats wholesale.

**Regular wholesale (local):** $3.50 product + $0.15 freight = **$3.65/sq ft delivered.** The baseline.

The nearby closeout is a clear win. The distant closeout barely beats regular wholesale.

For tile, the math is more dramatic:

Same exercise with tile at $1.80/sq ft — a heavier product:

**Tile closeout 100 miles:** $1.80 product + $0.40 freight = **$2.20/sq ft delivered.** Significant savings.

**Tile closeout 800 miles:** $1.80 product + $1.40 freight = **$3.20/sq ft delivered.** Saves almost nothing.

**Regular wholesale (local):** $3.00 product + $0.25 freight = **$3.25/sq ft delivered.** The baseline.

The distant tile closeout saves almost nothing. The local closeout saves significantly.

## How to Source Regionally

### Filter by Location

On B2B marketplaces like PlankMarket, filter listings by seller location. Prioritize:
- Same state
- Within 300 miles
- Same region (Southeast, Midwest, etc.)

### Know Your Distribution Hubs

Flooring distribution concentrates in certain cities:

- **Southeast** — Atlanta, Charlotte, Jacksonville
- **Northeast** — Baltimore, Philadelphia, Boston
- **Midwest** — Chicago, Cleveland, Indianapolis
- **Southwest** — Dallas, Houston, Phoenix
- **West** — Los Angeles, Seattle, Denver

Closeouts near distribution hubs often have better freight options and more inventory.

### Local Distributor Relationships

Your local distributors have closeouts they may not advertise. Ask directly:
- "What are you sitting on that needs to move?"
- "Anything discontinued you want to clear?"
- "Can I see your clearance inventory?"

Local pickup eliminates freight entirely.

### Consolidate When Possible

If you need volume, try to consolidate orders:
- Multiple products from same seller
- Coordinate with other contractors
- Build toward full truckload quantities

Full truckload from 800 miles away beats LTL.

## Evaluating Distant Closeouts

Sometimes distant closeouts are still worth pursuing. When:

**The discount is extreme.** A 60% discount can absorb freight costs and still be valuable.

**You need volume.** Full truckload rates make long-distance viable.

**The product is rare.** Exotic hardwood or specialty products may not be available locally.

**You have time.** Rail freight or economy LTL can reduce costs if you're not in a hurry.

Before committing to distant closeouts:

1. Get actual freight quotes (not estimates)
2. Calculate total delivered cost
3. Compare to local options (including regular wholesale)
4. Factor in lead time and risk

## The Calculation

Before any closeout purchase:

**Total Delivered Cost = Product Cost + Freight + Any Handling Fees**

Compare this to:
- Regular wholesale (delivered)
- Alternative closeouts (delivered)
- Local options even at higher product cost

The lowest product cost doesn't always mean the best deal. Total delivered cost is what matters.

## Practical Application

**For buyers:**
- Always get freight quotes before committing
- Prioritize local/regional closeouts
- Calculate delivered cost, not just product cost
- Consider pickup options when possible

**For sellers:**
- List your location prominently
- Offer pickup/will-call when possible
- Consider regional pricing (buyers nearby will pay more because freight is less)
- Partner with freight brokers for competitive rates

## Bottom Line

The best closeout deals are often nearby, not cheap.

A 30% discount with no freight beats a 40% discount with heavy freight. Prioritize regional sourcing, calculate delivered costs, and don't let freight turn a good deal into an average one.
