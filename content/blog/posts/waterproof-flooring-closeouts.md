# Waterproof Flooring Closeouts: LVP, WPC, and SPC Deals

Waterproof flooring has dominated the market for the past five years. That growth also created oversupply.

Distributors overbought. Manufacturers expanded production. Now there's surplus. For contractors, this means waterproof flooring closeouts are abundant and often deeply discounted.

This guide covers what's available, what to look for, and how to evaluate waterproof flooring closeouts.

## Understanding Waterproof Flooring Types

"Waterproof flooring" isn't one product. It's a category with distinct types:

### SPC (Stone Plastic Composite)

**Construction:** Limestone/calcium carbonate core with vinyl top layer
**Key traits:**
- Very rigid and dense
- Handles temperature changes well
- Best for uneven subfloors
- Clicks together tightly

**Current market position:** Most in-demand waterproof option. SPC closeouts are valuable.

### WPC (Wood Plastic Composite)

**Construction:** Foamed plastic core with vinyl top layer
**Key traits:**
- Softer underfoot than SPC
- Better acoustic properties
- Slightly less rigid
- Warmer feel

**Current market position:** Still popular but SPC has taken share. WPC closeouts may need deeper discounts.

### Rigid Core LVP

**Construction:** Various rigid cores (some proprietary)
**Key traits:**
- Brand-specific variations
- Performance depends on specific construction
- Often includes attached underlayment

**Current market position:** Varies by brand. Evaluate each product individually.

### Standard LVP

**Construction:** Flexible vinyl layers
**Key traits:**
- Thinner, more flexible
- Requires smooth subfloor
- Lower price point
- Less dimensional stability

**Current market position:** Entry-level option. Closeout pricing should reflect lower baseline.

## What to Evaluate

### Core Type

For closeout purchasing, SPC generally holds more value than WPC, which holds more value than standard LVP.

Know what you're buying. Marketing terms vary; actual construction matters.

### Total Thickness

| Thickness | Quality Level | Application |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Under 5mm | Entry | Light residential |
| 5-6mm | Standard | General residential |
| 6-8mm | Premium | Heavy residential, light commercial |
| 8mm+ | Commercial | Commercial applications |

Thicker is generally better for durability and sound absorption.

### Wear Layer

The wear layer protects against scratches and wear. Measured in mils:

| Wear Layer | Durability | Application |
|------------|------------|-------------|
| 6-12 mil | Light duty | Low-traffic residential |
| 12-20 mil | Standard | General residential |
| 20-28 mil | Heavy duty | High-traffic, light commercial |
| 28+ mil | Commercial | Commercial traffic |

For contractor work, 12 mil minimum. 20 mil for kitchens, entries, commercial.

### Attached Underlayment

Many waterproof products include attached padding:

**With attached pad:**
- Faster installation
- Consistent performance
- Premium pricing

**Without pad:**
- Requires separate underlayment
- Lower initial cost
- More installation steps

Attached pad products are worth more at closeout.

### Certifications

Look for:
- FloorScore (indoor air quality)
- GreenGuard (low emissions)
- CARB Phase 2 (formaldehyde)

Unknown manufacturers without certifications carry risk.

## Why Waterproof Closeouts Are Abundant

The waterproof flooring market is oversupplied because:

**Rapid growth attracted production.** Everyone saw the demand. Everyone expanded.

**Many manufacturers entered.** The category went from a few players to dozens.

**Style proliferation.** Too many colors, too many SKUs, spread demand thin.

**Post-2021 normalization.** Pandemic remodeling demand dropped. Inventory remained.

This supply/demand imbalance benefits buyers. Good product at significant discounts.

## Color and Style Considerations

Waterproof flooring is more style-sensitive than hardwood:

**Gray tones:** Dominated 2018-2022. Still selling but less in demand. May need deeper discounts.

**Warm browns:** Trending upward. Closeouts in warm tones move faster.

**Blonde/light:** Perennial demand. Usually moves at standard closeout pricing.

**Heavy texture/distressed:** Depends on current market. Some patterns feel dated quickly.

Consider what your clients are asking for. Closeouts in current styles are more valuable.

## Red Flags Specific to Waterproof Flooring

**Unverified waterproof claims.** Not all LVP is waterproof. Verify core construction.

**Thin wear layer marketed as "commercial."** Commercial requires 20+ mil. If specs don't support the claim, be skeptical.

**"Waterproof" laminate confusion.** Some products are laminate with water-resistant treatment, not true waterproof LVP/SPC. Different products with different limitations.

**No manufacturer identification.** Quality varies dramatically in this category. Unknown origin is higher risk.
