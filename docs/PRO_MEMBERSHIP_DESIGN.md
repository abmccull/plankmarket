# PlankMarket Pro Membership — Complete Design

**Date:** March 9, 2026
**Status:** Strategic Decision

---

## The Philosophy

Pro isn't an "AI add-on." It's **the serious user's PlankMarket**. Every benefit should make a serious buyer or seller think: "this is obviously worth $29." The AI agent is one piece, but the financial benefits alone should justify the price for anyone doing real volume.

The free tier should be genuinely useful — not a crippled demo. A first-time seller listing 5 pallets of surplus should have a great experience for free. But someone running a business on PlankMarket should feel Pro is table stakes.

#### The Financial Benefits (This Is What Sells Pro)

**1. Reduced Fees**

| Fee | Free | Pro | Savings at $5K/month | Savings at $10K/month |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buyer fee | 3.0% | **2.0%** | $50/month | $100/month |
| Seller fee | 2.0% | **1.0%** | $50/month | $100/month |

At **$3,000/month in transactions**, the fee reduction saves $30/month — Pro pays for itself.
At **$5,000/month**, it saves $50/month — Pro is free plus $21/month profit.
At **$10,000/month**, it saves $100/month — Pro returns 3.5x.

This is the hook. Any user doing $3K+/month in volume has a mathematical reason to subscribe. They don't need to understand AI or care about automation — the math just works.

**2. Reduced Shipping Costs**

| | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Shipping markup | 25% over carrier rate | **15% over carrier rate** |

On a $1,200 shipping bill (typical for LTL freight):
- Free: Carrier charges $960, you pay $1,200. PlankMarket margin: $240.
- Pro: Carrier charges $960, you pay $1,104. PlankMarket margin: $144. **You save $96.**

On a $2,000 shipping bill:
- Free: You pay $2,000. Pro: You pay $1,840. **Saves $160.**

For a buyer making 2-3 purchases per month with shipping, this alone can save $200-500/month.

**3. Monthly Promotion Credit**

| | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Promotion credit | $0 | **$25/month** (use it or lose it) |

$25 covers a 7-day Spotlight boost ($29 value). Sellers who promote regularly save $300/year. More importantly, this gets sellers *trying* promotions who otherwise wouldn't. Promoted listings sell faster → more transactions → more fee revenue for PlankMarket.

**4. Faster Payouts**

| | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Payout timing | 5 business days after delivery | **Next business day** after delivery |

This is massive for cash-flow-sensitive businesses. A seller shipping 5 orders/month at $3K each has $15K in escrow at any time. Getting that money 4 days sooner is worth real money (working capital cost). This benefit costs PlankMarket very little — Stripe transfers are near-instant — but the perceived value is enormous.

#### The AI Agent Benefits (Pro Exclusive)

**10. Autonomous Offer Handling**

Set rules. Agent handles offers 24/7:
- Accept above X% of ask
- Counter at Y% if offer is between X% and Z%
- Reject below Z%
- Custom message templates per action

**11. Autonomous Listing Monitoring** (buyers)

Agent scans for matching listings every few hours during business hours. Alerts you when something matches. Optionally makes offers automatically within your price parameters.

**12. Smart Repricing** (sellers)

Agent adjusts stale listings based on engagement data and market comparisons. Rules-based: "drop 5% after 14 days with no offers, never below $X." You set the floor, agent optimizes above it.

**13. Auto-RFQ Matching** (sellers)

Agent monitors buyer requests and responds with matching inventory automatically.

**14. Negotiation Autopilot**

Agent handles counter-offer rounds per your rules. A 3-day back-and-forth becomes a 3-hour conversation.

**15. Value Dashboard**

Your agent's report card: time saved, deals facilitated, money saved on fees/shipping, market insights delivered. Updated monthly. Shows the ROI in plain dollars.

## What This Means for PlankMarket Revenue

### Revenue Impact Per Pro User

Let's model a typical Pro seller doing $8K/month:

| Revenue Line | Free User | Pro User | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seller fee | $160 (2%) | $80 (1%) | **-$80** |
| Shipping margin | $480 (25%) | $288 (15%) | **-$192** |
| Promotion spending | $0 avg | $50 avg (net of $25 credit) | **+$50** |
| Pro subscription | $0 | $29 | **+$29** |
| **Platform revenue** | **$640** | **$447** | **-$193** |

Wait — **that looks like we lose money converting a free user to Pro.** But this is the wrong frame. Here's why:

### The Revenue Frame That Matters

**1. Pro users transact more.**

A seller with auto-offer handling closes deals they would have missed (slow response = lost buyer). Smart repricing moves stale inventory. RFQ matching creates new deals. Conservative estimate: **Pro sellers do 20-30% more transaction volume** because the platform works harder for them.

Adjusted numbers for a Pro seller:

| Revenue Line | Free ($8K GMV) | Pro ($10K GMV, +25%) | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seller fee | $160 (2%) | $100 (1%) | -$60 |
| Shipping margin | $480 (25%) | $360 (15%) | -$120 |
| Promotion spending | $0 | $50 | +$50 |
| Subscription | $0 | $29 | +$29 |
| **Platform revenue** | **$640** | **$539** | **-$101** |

Still negative if the volume increase is only 25%. Let's recalibrate the fee discount.

### Recalibrated Fee Discount

The fee discount is too aggressive at 1% buyer / 1% seller. Let's find the sweet spot where Pro is still a no-brainer but PlankMarket doesn't lose money:

**Option A: Moderate discount**

| Fee | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer fee | 3.0% | **2.5%** |
| Seller fee | 2.0% | **1.5%** |

At $8K/month seller: saves $40/month on fees (not $80). Combined with shipping savings ($192) and promotion credit ($25), total savings = $257. ROI = 8.9x. Still a no-brainer.

Platform revenue at $8K GMV:
| | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Seller fee | $160 | $120 |
| Shipping margin | $480 | $288 |
| Promotions | $0 | $50 |
| Subscription | $0 | $29 |
| **Total** | **$640** | **$487** |

At +25% volume ($10K GMV):
| | Pro ($10K) |
|---|---|
| Seller fee | $150 |
| Shipping margin | $360 |
| Promotions | $50 |
| Subscription | $29 |
| **Total** | **$589** |

Still slightly below $640. But we haven't counted the buyer-side benefits...

**The other side of the transaction matters.** If the buyer is also Pro, PlankMarket collects:
- Buyer fee: 2.5% of $10K = $250 (vs 3% of $8K = $240 from a free buyer)
- Buyer's Pro sub: $29

**Full transaction economics (both sides Pro, $10K order):**
- Seller fee: $150
- Buyer fee: $250
- Shipping margin: $360
- Two Pro subs: $58
- Promotions: $50
- **Total: $868** vs $880 from two free users on an $8K order

Nearly break-even on a per-transaction basis, but with higher GMV. And the compounding effects matter:

**2. Pro users retain better.**

A user saving $200+/month on fees and shipping doesn't leave. Their agent is configured, their data is building, their CRM is populated. Switching cost is high, perceived value is high.

Free user churn: assume 8-10%/month.
Pro user churn: assume 3-4%/month.

Over 12 months, a Pro user generates 2-3x the lifetime revenue of a free user because they stick around.

**3. Pro users attract other users.**

Pro sellers respond faster (auto-offers), price better (market intelligence), and have better listings (AI creation + promotion credits). This makes the buying experience better, which attracts more buyers, which attracts more sellers. Marketplace flywheel.

## What We're NOT Doing

| Temptation | Why Not |
|---|---|
| Multiple tiers (Pro, Business, Enterprise) | Market doesn't support it. Two tiers is clean and honest. |
| Per-action pricing | Creates anxiety, hurts adoption. |
| Transaction caps on free tier | Directly kills marketplace liquidity. Never gate a transaction. |
| Messaging limits | Kills deal flow. Let people talk. |
| Commission-only model (no subscription) | Fee discounts are the strongest Pro benefit — need a subscription to gate them. |
| Free trial of Pro | $29 is low enough that trials aren't needed. But consider a 7-day refund guarantee. |
| Freemium AI with heavy limits | AI listing creation and search should be free — they make the platform better for everyone, including non-Pro users. |
